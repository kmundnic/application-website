Abstract

This work presents the implementation of early high volatility cluster detectors based on uGARCH models (a variation of the GARCH model), particle- filtering-based state estimators, and hypothesis testing procedures. The detection scheme uses prior and posterior probability estimates to build asymmetric hypothesis tests that detect sudden volatility increments (typically associated with early stages of high-volatility clusters). Performance of the classic particle filter is compared to a risk-sensitive version that aims at tracking regions of the state-space associated to a greater financial risk.
