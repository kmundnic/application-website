Research Assistantship 2

Sat 31 December 2011 | tags: project-portfolio, research, -- (permalink)

  • Project title: Exploration of the use of the Kullback-Leibler divergence in statistical models comparison
  • FONDECYT Grant Number: 1110145.
  • FONDECYT Project Title: "Universal Estimation of Information Measures and Applications" (2011-2013).
  • Principal Investigator: Jorge F. Silva, Faculty of Mathematical and Physical Sciences, Department of Electrical Engineering, Universidad de Chile.

Abstract

This work addresses the use of Information Theoretic measures in statistical learning. Specifically, the Kullback-Leibler divergence was used to compare models of varying complexity, including histograms, autoregressive models, and markov chains. The area of application was self modeling robotics: The data was obtained from a simulator where one can extract data from the movement of robots (for example, movement of the mass center and movements of limbs, among others).

Work

  1. Write code in Matlab® to calculate the Kullback-Leibler divergence between two probability density functions with the same support.
  2. Write code in Matlab® to calculate the Kullback-Leibler divergence between probability density functions of different support (using a kernel approach).
  3. Write code in Matlab® to calculate the Kullback-Leibler divergence between autoregressive models with Gaussian noise.
  4. Write code in Matlab® to calculate the Kullback-Leibler divergence between markov chains of varying dimensionality.
  5. Adjust the simulator written in C++ to obtain necessary data.
